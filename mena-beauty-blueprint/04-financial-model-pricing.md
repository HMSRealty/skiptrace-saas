# MODULE 4 — Financial Model & Pricing Strategy

> All figures are **planning models** in USD for comparability. Convert at launch and adjust to real supplier/ad costs. Rough FX anchors used: 1 USD ≈ 3.75 SAR ≈ 3.67 AED ≈ 48 EGP (verify current rates).

---

## 1. Pricing Formula — Gulf (premium) vs Egypt (value)

### Core markup targets
- **Skincare/serums:** 3–4× COGS
- **Perfumes/mists:** 3.5–5× COGS (high perceived value)
- **Beauty tools:** 2.5–3.5× COGS (higher absolute price, lower multiple)

### Gulf — Premium / charm pricing psychology
- Price to **signal luxury**: round-up "prestige" pricing — e.g. **SAR 199, AED 249, SAR 299** (not 197.50). Round, confident numbers read as premium.
- Use **anchoring**: show a "كان 399 ر.س" crossed-out price next to "الآن 249 ر.س".
- **Bundle to raise AOV** (BNPL via Tabby/Tamara makes high tickets feel small: "ادفعيها على ٤ دفعات").
- Free shipping threshold to push basket size (e.g. "شحن مجاني فوق 200 ر.س").

### Egypt — Value-based / charm pricing psychology
- Price ends in **9 / 99** to read as a deal — e.g. **399 ج.م, 499 ج.م, 749 ج.م**.
- Emphasize **COD + "ادفع عند الاستلام"** and **value bundles** ("اشتري ٢ واحصلي على خصم").
- Keep entry price points accessible; lead with the deal/discount in ad creative.
- Build margin into price to absorb **COD refusal/return cost** (price ~10–15% higher to cover refused-order shipping losses).

> **Same product, two prices.** Use Shopify Markets to set market-specific prices. Gulf customers pay a premium; Egypt gets value pricing. Never show the same number to both.

### C) Beauty Tool (cordless hair styler)
| Line | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| Selling price | $89.00 |
| COGS (product) | $34.00 |
| Shipping/fulfillment (heavier item) | $7.00 |
| Payment fees (~3%) | $2.67 |
| Ad spend / CPA | $22.00 |
| Misc (packaging, returns reserve ~5%) | $4.45 |
| **Net profit / unit** | **≈ $18.88 (21% margin)** |

**Read-out:**
- Perfumes give the **best %margin** → lead retention/repeat purchase here.
- Tools give the **biggest absolute profit + AOV** → great for BNPL + upsell, but watch return/defect risk (electronics).
- Serums are the **easiest first sale + reorder** (consumable, builds LTV).
- **Blended target: keep CPA below ~⅓ of price and net margin ≥25%.** Push AOV with bundles to improve unit economics.

> ⚠️ Egypt economics differ: lower price points + COD refusal losses → thinner per-unit margin but higher volume. Model Egypt separately with a **refusal-rate line (10–25%)** baked into CPA.

| **Buffer** | Returns/refusals + misc | $150 |
| | **TOTAL (realistic minimum)** | **≈ $1,500 – $2,000** |

**Notes:**
- **Absolute bare-minimum** (pure dropship test, no local stock): ~$700–900 — but you lose the liquid/logistics moat and delivery is slow.
- **Recommended starting budget for a real shot:** **$2,000–$3,000**, with **~50–60% of it reserved for ad testing** — creative testing volume, not store polish, is what finds the winner.
- Don't over-invest in inventory before validating winners. Test dropship → bulk-stock only proven winners.
- Reinvest first 2–3 months of profit fully back into ads + inventory; expect to be near break-even while you find winning products and creatives.
